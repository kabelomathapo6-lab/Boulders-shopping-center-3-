Hi there,

My name is Kabelo Mathapo. I'm a software engineering student at Melsoft Academy in
Rosebank, and The Boulders is my local centre — I shop there regularly.

As part of a course project, we were asked to pick a real business, study its website,
and rebuild it as a learning exercise. I chose The Boulders, and I wanted to share the
result with you rather than just hand it in and forget about it.

You can see it here: [YOUR LIVE LINK]

A few things I focused on:

- **Searchable store directory.** You can type "Clicks" or "Steers" and get the shop
  number instantly, rather than scrolling the full list.
- **A live "open now" status**, so someone can tell at a glance whether it's worth
  driving over.
- **Mobile-first wayfinding**, since most people check a mall's website from their phone.
- **Search-engine basics.** At the moment each page returns a title of "Redefine Retail"
  with no description, so Google shows no summary for boulders.co.za. Adding a proper
  title and description per page would help the centre rank for its own name.

To be clear, this is an unofficial student project, not a pitch and not a criticism of
your team — I know a centre website has constraints I can't see from the outside. I just
learned a lot building it and thought the people who run the place might find it
interesting.

If any of it is useful to you, you're welcome to it. And if you ever have questions about
it, I'm happy to chat.

Thanks for your time, and for a centre my family has used for years.
